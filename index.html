Personal profile outlining my goals & achievements

Things I want to do

There are countless things I'd love to learn and do this year, but I have decided to focus my attention on six key areas:

  • Develop copywriting skills, focusing on micro copy.
  • Practice public speaking at more events.
  • Work on methods of communicating ideas to co-workers and clients.
  • Do more physical prototyping, from foam modelling to programming Arduinos.
  • Work towards organising larger conferences with high professional standards.
  • Design for multi-screen experiences.

I have set myself a lot of goals and I have a lot to learn. Fortunately, I know there are so many people in the community who helped me in the past and I'm sure I will meet many more this year. If you can help push me in the right direction or have advice to offer on any of the above, I can assure you it will truly be appreciated.

Things I have done

Over the past few years I've had the pleasure of working on some pretty cool projects with equally awesome people. I've written about some of the personal highlights below.

Played with Robbie Williams face

I worked with Google and Universal to design and build the Robbie Williams' Google Hangout app. Users got to play with the artwork of Robbie's latest album via face tracking and some custom tools we put together. You can see what others did or play with it yourself on Google Plus .

Been interviewed

It was far from being Question Time, but I got to talk about subjects I really love and care about, so even if only a handful of people found it useful, that still put a smile on my face. You can see the interviews here: Lanyrd Blog - How to run an evening event and UBelly - Mobile UI and fried chicken.

Created a community

In the spring of 2011, Daniel Knell and I had the idea of creating a new tech event. The goals were simple: offer something for the best and brightest web developers around, and create a space to share and stimulate ideas. Two years later London JS has had 42 speakers from all over the world coming to share their knowledge with our community. Some personal highlights for me have been Paul King, Irene Ros, Addy Osmani, Syd Lawrence, Mark Wubben, Jake Archibald and Andrew Betts. I've had the pleasure of watching some of our new faces develop into regulars of the conference circuit and seen our attendees get inspired to make some wonderful things.

I had cocktails

I've spent a fair bit of time working at agencies over the years and all my start-up friends tell me how dull and corporate that sounds. Sure, I worked on marketing emails and finance websites, but over the years I also got to work on some pretty cool projects with some of the best developers. These projects ranged from building mobile shopping apps to prototyping Wii apps and designing 50-foot wide touch interfaces. To top it all off, I was a part of a team tasked with designing a cocktail in two days. It was geeky, it was messy, it was like Mad Men meets the IT Crowd.

...This tastes like the mid 90's
